The strategic picture — legislative demand vs. ULAP alignment

Each bubble is a distinct reform proposal, sized by how many measures it bundles, placed by how many House bills push it (vertical, log scale) against how well it aligns with ULAP's six principles (horizontal). The most-filed reforms are not the most principled: several high-demand proposals sit in the red zone — popular, yet failing ULAP's tests (mandatory-officer mandates, term extensions, re-nationalizing devolved functions), while the strongest-aligned reforms attract less legislative push.
